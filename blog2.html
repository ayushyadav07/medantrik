"TRAP" The curse of Developing Nation

Reading Time: 5 minutes
  • Almost 90% of people in India do not have cars.
  • Traffic-Related Air Pollution (TRAP) poses significant health dangers.
  • Results in respiratory problems and cardiovascular diseases, particularly in densely populated urban areas.
  • More than 90% of pollution-related deaths occurred in low-income and middle-income countries, with India topping the list with 2.36 million (Lancet Study).

What is TRAP ?

TRAP” stands for “Traffic-Related Air Pollution.” It refers to the pollution of the air caused by vehicles and traffic-related activities.

How this effects India

  • India had 32.63 crore vehicles in 2020 and nearly 75% of these were two-wheelers with a growth rate of 13.6% per year.
  • India had approx 20 crore roadside business more than 50% of the Indian schools are in the side of main road.
